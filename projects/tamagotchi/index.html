Since the start of Autumn 2021 quarter, I have been a Research Assistant for the Tamagotchi Robots project in Dr. Sarah Sebo’s Human-Robot Interaction Lab.

Under Dr. Sebo’s direction, the motivation behind our research is to determine whether the long-term care of a social companion robot will improve the interpersonal connection between human and robot and improve the likelihood of interaction. For our purposes, we are using the Anki Vector robot (see the little guy below).

Our main tech stack is Python3 and Robot Operating System (ROS), and we’re also using a Raspberry Pi 3B+ to help manage our custom behaviors for Vector.

(Taken by labmate Spencer Ng)

Currently, we are in the stage of developing the technological chassis and experimental protocol, in preparation for a pilot study conducted at the end of the Winter 2022 quarter.

Though the roots of this project date back to late 2020, the bulk of our (my and the other undergrads') progress has been made in this quarter and will continue into the rest of the year.
